About

I am a Geomatics Engineer and GIS Specialist based in Siakago, Kenya, holding a B.Sc. in Geomatics Engineering and Geospatial Information Systems (Second Class Honours, Upper Division) from Dedan Kimathi University of Technology, 2025. I work at the intersection of field surveying, spatial analysis, and backend software development.

My day-to-day spans cadastral and topographical surveying at the Mbeere North Sub-County Survey Office, building spatial web platforms — most recently HydroGPT, a water accessibility tool combining PostGIS network analysis with an AI natural-language query interface — and tutoring GIS students remotely across Kenya, the US, and Canada.

I am drawn to problems where geospatial thinking meets software: automating repetitive survey office workflows, applying remote sensing to environmental monitoring, or building full-stack applications that make spatial data accessible to non-technical users. I pick up tools quickly and thrive in environments that value precision and initiative.
