Skills & Artifacts

Problem-Solving & Diagnostics

One of my proudest experiences was diagnosing a difficult engine misfire issue that had stumped other shops. I used critical thinking and persistence to identify the failing fuel injector, successfully resolving the issue. This reinforced my problem-solving skills and patience under pressure. Rebuilding a full engine further tested my ability to follow technical specs and overcome frustration, while applying logic and precision to every step. These experiences strengthened my identity as a technician, and I now realize how vital these traits are as I transition into leadership roles in future business ventures.

Communication & Customer Service

Explaining technical repairs to customers in simple terms has sharpened my communication skills. Whether providing cost estimates or walking someone through a service issue, I've learned the value of clarity and empathy. During a community event, I also led volunteers and handled logistics, using teamwork and negotiation to keep the project on track. These experiences show I can connect with people, manage relationships, and earn trust — all essential for business success.

Adaptability & Lifelong Learning

The automotive industry is constantly evolving. I've actively sought to stay current by learning about hybrid/electric vehicle systems and advanced diagnostic software. This growth mindset has helped me build confidence in tackling unfamiliar challenges. Additionally, my reflections on Indigenous knowledge and ways of knowing taught me to approach learning holistically. I've learned that knowledge is shaped by environment and experience—key concepts that now influence how I study, lead, and grow. I plan to build on this by attending workshops and seeking further training.
