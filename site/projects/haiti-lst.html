Haiti Land Surface Temperature

2024  ·  Remote Sensing  ·  QGIS · Landsat 8 · Thermal Band Analysis

Land surface temperature (LST) mapping of Haiti using Landsat 8 thermal infrared imagery. The analysis quantifies spatial variation in surface heat, revealing the thermal signature of urban areas, deforested zones, and vegetation cover across the country.

Land surface temperature — Haiti. Derived from Landsat 8 Band 10 (TIRS). Colour ramp: cool (blue) to hot (red/white).

Background

Haiti has experienced significant deforestation and urban expansion over recent decades. LST mapping provides a measurable proxy for the effects of land cover change — urban heat islands form where vegetation is lost and impervious surfaces dominate. This analysis was conducted as part of broader remote sensing coursework exploring thermal remote sensing workflows.

Methodology

  • Acquired Landsat 8 Level-2 imagery (surface temperature product, Band 10)
  • Applied radiometric calibration and atmospheric correction constants
  • Converted top-of-atmosphere radiance to brightness temperature in Kelvin, then Celsius
  • Derived emissivity correction using NDVI-based fractional vegetation estimates
  • Produced a classified LST map with a diverging colour ramp for clear thermal interpretation

Key Findings

  • Port-au-Prince and surrounding urban zones registered the highest surface temperatures (>42°C)
  • Deforested hillside areas showed markedly elevated LST compared to forested equivalents
  • Coastal wetlands and water bodies provided the lowest surface temperatures, acting as thermal buffers
  • Clear spatial correlation between low NDVI and high LST confirms vegetation-temperature relationship

Tools

  • QGIS — image processing, band math, cartographic output
  • Landsat 8 TIRS (Band 10) — thermal infrared source imagery
  • USGS EarthExplorer — imagery acquisition
